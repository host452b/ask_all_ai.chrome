# Philosophy

> One question, every AI.

## Why AskAll exists

**Equal Sampling** — the same question, sent to every AI, at the same time, with no preference.

In the age of search, your job was to **ask the right question**.
In the age of AI, every chatbot hands you a confident answer — and that is precisely when you are most likely to stop thinking.

AskAll turns AI from an **oracle** into a **witness**.

One witness speaks and you might accept everything.
Twenty-three witnesses speak at once and you instinctively begin to:

- Notice what **one says that the others don't**
- Notice what **all of them agree on** — likely close to fact
- Notice where **they contradict each other** — where the real complexity hides
- And finally, **make your own judgment**

This is dialectics you can use every day — not in a textbook, but in a browser tab.

## Core beliefs

1. **No single intelligence holds the complete truth.** Every AI carries the biases of its training data, the preferences of its alignment, and the blind spots of its architecture.

2. **Clarity emerges from contradiction.** When Claude and GPT give opposite answers, you are just beginning to approach the essence of the question.

3. **Human value lies not in asking, but in judging.** The stronger AI becomes, the more irreplaceable the human role as the final arbiter.

4. **Equal sampling beats cherry-picking.** When you only ask one AI, you get one bias. When you ask all of them equally, biases cancel out and signal survives.

5. **Diversity is the immune system of cognition.** Just as biodiversity protects ecosystems, intellectual diversity protects thinking.

## The shift

| Era | Human role | Tool | Risk |

One question, every AI — equal sampling across all intelligences, so you can be the judge.
